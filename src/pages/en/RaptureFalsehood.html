Rapture Falsehood

To believe or not in the "Rapture" before the Great Tribulation could become a matter of life or death, a matter of torture or liberty

    There is going to be an ingathering of the Christians during the Second Coming, but not a “rapture” before the Great Tribulation. The brethren that believe in a Rapture previous to the Great Tribulation should analyze very carefully this issue, because it can become a matter of life or death, of torture or liberty, for themselves and their family. It is like having upon them the blood of their family members, for the foolishness of not searching in detail, without the prejudice or emotion caused by a desire to believe in this imaginary Rapture.

    If these brothers are preachers, then they have a double responsibility, because in addition to their own family, they will have the blood of those they deceive upon them, and for whose deception those unhappy ones would be tortured and murdered during the Great Tribulation. A most terrible weight on the conscience for those who preach the false doctrine of the Rapture!

    I warn you clearly about the falsehood of that heretical interpretation of “a Rapture before the Great Tribulation”. I demonstrate in my book “Rapture Vs. Ingathering” that it is erroneous, and with this I am free of the blood of those that want to close their eyes to the truth. The apostle Paul warned the churches of the Gentiles Thessalonians very clearly, so that they would not allow themselves to be deceived with doctrines like that of the Rapture before the Great Tribulation. Hence he knew that someone would try to deceive the brethren towards the latter days. In essence, by warning them he warned us too.

 

    “1 Now we beseech you, brethren, by the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, and by our gathering together unto him, 2 that ye be not soon shaken in mind, or be troubled, neither by spirit, nor by word, nor by letter as from us, as that the day of Christ is at hand. 3 Let no man deceive you by any means; for that day shall not come, except there come a falling away first, and that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition; 4 who opposeth and exalteth himself above all that is called God, or that is worshipped; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God.” (II Thes 2:1-4)

    It is very different the risk of those that believe in the Rapture before the Great Tribulation, and those that believe in the Ingathering during the Second Coming of Christ. If there were a Rapture before the Great Tribulation, what would happen to the Christians that do not believe in it? Nothing would happen to them; they would be raptured along with the other brothers that do believe in the Rapture.

But if they were alert, they would think that they would not need to do anything other than wait some more to be raptured. The terrible harm that this false doctrine of the Rapture will cause the brethren is that they will not leave their comforts and move to the “wilderness” where God has determined that they be taken care of. Instead they are going to stay waiting where they can be captured, always thinking that the Rapture is imminent.
